The Louvre Museum

The Louvre Museum is the most important in the country and one of the most visited in the world, with more than eight million people visiting its collections every year.

Musée d'Orsay

Is an art gallery located in Paris (France) dedicated to the visual arts of the nineteenth century and, more specifically, of the period 1848-1914.

Notre Dame

The cathedral of Notre Dame, or Notre-Dame de Paris, represents the French Gothic style in all its glory. Construction began in 1163, and since then, it has been a fundamental reference in Western culture. For this reason, this cathedral is part of the UNESCO World Heritage catalogue.

The Alexander III Bridge

The Alexander III Bridge symbolizes Art Nouveau, an architectural style that has greatly influenced the arts and sculpture. If you walk past it, you'll notice that it's full of detail, with shapes symbolizing plants, flowers, animals, and mystical objects, such as nymphs and cherubs, with Renaissance lanterns and winged horses at both ends.

Luxembourg Gardens

Paris is a city of large green spaces and one of the most well-kept and also one of the most visited are the Luxembourg Gardens, south of the Seine River. Its design, fountains and flowers are worth a visit, but don't just go to do the sightseeing check: sit down to read, buy an ice cream and watch the daily life of Paris move in this park.
