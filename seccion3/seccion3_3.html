Leicester Square

Is a pedestrianised square in the West End of London, England. It was laid out in 1670 as Leicester Fields, which was named after the recently built Leicester House, itself named after Robert Sidney, 2nd Earl of Leicester.

Buckingham Palace

Built in 1703 for the Duke of Buckingham, Buckingham Palace replaced St James's Palace as the monarch's official London residence in 1837. Queen Elizabeth II divides her time between here, Windsor Castle and, in summer, Balmoral Castle in Scotland.

Tower Bridge

It crosses the River Thames close to the Tower of London and is one of five London bridges owned and maintained by the City Bridge Foundation, a charitable trust founded in 1282. The bridge was constructed to give better access to the East End of London, which had expanded its commercial potential in the 19th century.

London’s Financial Districts

The Square Mile, or the City of London (“the City”), represents the historical core and houses major financial institutions, including the Bank of England and the London Stock Exchange. Lombard Street, located within the City, holds historical significance and is associated with banking and finance.

London Eye

The London Eye was inaugurated on December 31, 1999, in a ceremony led by the then British Prime Minister Tony Blair. It was only opened to the public in March of the following year and to date it has become an attractive point for London tourism.
