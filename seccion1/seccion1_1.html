Renaissance

David

The sculpture represents the biblical King David in the moment before facing Goliath, and was welcomed as a symbol of the Republic of Florence in the face of the hegemony of its overthrown rulers, the Medici, and the threat of the adjacent states, especially the Papal States.

The Last Judgement

The picture radiates out from the centre figure of Christ, and Michelangelo has chosen to depict the various saints included in the work holding the instruments of their martyrdom rather than the actual scenes of torture.

The Betrayal of Christ

a scene from his fresco cycle of the Life of Christ in the Arena Chapel in Padua, the naturalism of his figures and their layered composition is turned up a notch to let the dramatic narrative unfold in a more naturalistic space. The figures have a greater three-dimensional form, more expressive body language and eye contact, with a particular focus on the revealing look between Jesus and Judas, just at the moment of his betrayal.

Trevi Fountain

The Trevi Fountain was not only created to be a work of art but also a political statement. It was commissioned by Pope Clement XII, who wanted to showcase the power and influence of the Catholic Church in Rome.

Renaissance Architecture

Imitating the works of great Greek and Roman architects, Renaissance-era structures often feature simple geometrical shapes - rectangles, domes, spirals, rows of columns, and arcades - that were in stark contrast with the irregular shapes of Gothic buildings.
